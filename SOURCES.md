# Sources for Fox Sister (여우 누이/狐姐)

## Primary Korean Sources

1. **금오신화 (Geumosinhwa / Tales of Mt. Geumo)** - Kim Sisŭp (15th century)
   - Earliest Korean literary work featuring supernatural themes
   - Foundation for later fox spirit literature
   - Available in modern Korean and English translations

2. **삼국유사 (Samguk Yusa / Memorabilia of the Three Kingdoms)** - Ilyeon (13th century)
   - Historical records including supernatural accounts
   - Early Korean spirit beliefs documented
   - Essential primary source for Korean folklore

3. **삼국사기 (Samguk Sagi / History of the Three Kingdoms)** - Kim Busik (12th century)
   - Historical chronicles with occasional supernatural elements
   - Context for early Korean beliefs

4. **조선왕조실록 (Joseon Wangjo Sillok / Annals of the Joseon Dynasty)**
   - Historical records mentioning fox spirit beliefs
   - Official dynastic records (1392-1910)
   - Available online through National Institute of Korean History

5. **한국구비문학대계 (Encyclopedia of Korean Oral Literature)**
   - Comprehensive collection of Korean folk tales
   - Regional fox sister variations
   - Academic compilation by multiple researchers
